Konark Sun Temple

Summary

The Konark Sun Temple in Odisha, built around 1250 CE by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga dynasty, is designed as a gigantic chariot of the Sun God Surya. With 24 elaborately carved wheels and 7 horses, the entire temple is an astronomical clock that tracks the movement of the sun with scientific precision.

Key Facts
  • The 24 wheels of the chariot each function as a sundial to tell the time
  • The temple was called the 'Black Pagoda' by European sailors who used it for navigation
  • Each wheel is 3 metres in diameter and carved with intricate designs
  • The seven horses represent the seven days of the week
  • The temple faces east so the first rays of sunrise illuminate the main idol
Why It Matters

Konark is where art, astronomy, and architecture meet. The Sun Temple is simultaneously a place of worship, an astronomical observatory, and a masterpiece of sculpture — combining spiritual and scientific knowledge in breathtaking stone.

Did You Know?

Sailors used to call Konark the 'Black Pagoda' while a nearby lighthouse was called the 'White Pagoda' — together they guided ships safely to the Odisha coast for centuries.
